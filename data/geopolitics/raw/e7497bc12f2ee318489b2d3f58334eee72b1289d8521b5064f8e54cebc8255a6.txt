SITUATION OVERVIEW 
• On 2 April, the Office of the RC/HC relocated to 
Khartoum following the recent re-establishment 
of government ministries. WFP Sudan is 
implementing a phased return to Khartoum and 
rotations supported by the regular UNHAS 
airbridge. 
• 15 April 2026 marked the third anniversary of the 
war in Sudan. In a statement, the UN 
Secretary-General warned that Sudan has 
become the world’s largest humanitarian crisis 
and urged an immediate ceasefire, civilian 
protection, full funding for aid, and a credible 
civilian-led political process. 
• At the Berlin Conference on 15 April, donors 
pledged over USD 1.5 billion to Sudan. 
• On 24 April, a truck carrying relief items 
from UNHCR came under drone attack in North 
Darfur, destroying emergency shelter supplies 
intended for 1,300 families in Tawila, where more 
than 700,000 displaced people have sought 
safety after fleeing fighting in other parts of 
Darfur.  
 
KEY HIGHLIGHTS 
• Since the beginning of 2026, WFP has been 
unable to achieve the monthly average reach of 
2025 (4 million). The decrease in reach has been 
driven by funding shortages, access constraints, 
and administrative delays. 
• In April, 798,000 people were reached in areas 
projected to be already facing or at risk of 
catastrophic hunger (IPC phase 4-5) covering 62 
percent of the 1.3 million food-insecure 
population in those locations.  
• Out of the 3.1 million people assisted in April, 
WFP reached 2.9 million crisis-affected people 
with food and cash assistance. 
• WFP also reached 1.1 million people through CBT 
in April.  
• WFP provided integrated nutrition support to 
203,000 people, including children under five and 
pregnant and breastfeeding women and girls 
(PBWG). 
• In Tawila, North Darfur, home to the largest 
population of internally displaced persons (IDPs), 
WFP delivered life-saving emergency food and 
nutrition assistance to more than 450,000 
people. 
• From 8 to 14 April, WFP Deputy Executive 
Director Carl Skau undertook a high-level mission 
to Sudan. The mission focused on engagement 
with government authorities, the reestablishment 
of WFP’s presence in Khartoum, and a review of 
operational conditions in Darfur and key border 
areas.

OPERATIONAL CHALLENGES  
• In April, WFP reached fewer beneficiaries than 
planned, due to a combination of challenges 
including acute funding shortfalls, active conflict 
and bureaucratic impediments.  
• In 2026 thus far, the CO has received USD 87.2 
million, just 13 percent of funding received 
during the same period in 2025, significantly 
constraining the scale and continuity of 
operations. 
• Active conflict and reoccurring drone strikes have 
prevented WFP from reaching people in the 
Kordofan and Darfur regions. This has resulted in 
a lack of WFP assistance for certain vulnerable 
populations. This includes Kornoi and Um Baru in 
North Darfur, two locations that have surpassed 
famine thresholds for Global Acute Malnutrition 
(GAM). This also includes Dilling (Risk of Famine 
Location) and Kadugli (Famine Location) in South 
Kordofan.  
• This continued insecurity in greater Kordofan has 
resulted in WFP being unable to reach West 
Kordofan, such as Al Lagowa and As Sunut, two 
risk of famine locations, with in-kind assistance 
since the start of 2026.  
• Over the past two months, delays in HAC 
approvals have substantially constrained WFP 
distributions. In April, 2,600 mt of food 
commodities remain pending movement 
clearance in Al Dabbah and Dongola, impacting 
assistance to 300,000 people, delaying WFP’s 
April distribution cycle across North Darfur, East 
Darfur, South Darfur, and Khartoum. 
Negotiations with the authorities are ongoing. 
• In addition, the impact of the Middle East Crisis is 
causing disruptions to maritime corridors which 
is stalling food, fuel and fertilizer imports, 
straining fragile supply chains, inflating delivery 
costs and constraining humanitarian operations.   
 
OPERATIONAL UPDATES 
Greater Kordofan 
• In April, active conflict affected supply routes 
resulting in prolonged convoy delays in the 
Kordofans. Despite this, WFP reached 387,000 
people (North Kordofan 235,000; South Kordofan 
152,000; and West Kordofan 200) across the 
Kordofans this month. 324,000 people were 
reached with in-kind food assistance and 50,000 
people were reached with CBT.  
• The security situation remained volatile, with 
drone strikes in North Kordofan and clashes 
along the Dilling–Kadugli route disrupting key 
corridors and supply movements.  
• At the same time, insecurity and inflation, 
including a reported 300% increase in sorghum 
prices in parts of South Kordofan, reduced both 
food availability and affordability. 
• Market disruptions impacted physical access to 
food and purchasing power was constrained. In 
response, in-kind food assistance was critical 
where markets broke down or access was 
blocked, while cash-based transfers (CBT) was 
effective where food was available but 
unaffordable.  
• WFP’s Shock-Responsive Emergency Safety Net 
programme reached 28,000 people with CBT in 
North Kordofan, where households are facing 
prolonged shocks.  
• In addition, under WFP’s crisis response, 22,000 
people in North and South Kordofan were 
reached with CBT in April. WFP also managed to 
reach 200 beneficiaries with CBT in West 
Kordofan (Al Lagowa and As Sunut), two risk of 
famine locations. 
• To mitigate the impact of Moderate Acute 
Malnutrition (MAM) in the Kordofans, WFP 
reached 11,000 children and pregnant and 
breastfeeding woman and girls (PBWG) 
supporting the treatment of MAM in North and 
South Kordofan. WFP also provided emergency 
blanket supplementary feeding for 270 
individuals in Kortala, South Kordofan, a risk of 
famine location.  
Greater Darfur 
• In April, WFP sustained large-scale operations 
across the Darfurs reaching 1.4 million people 
(Central Darfur 271,000; East Darfur 88,000; 
North Darfur 682,000; South Darfur 70,000; and 
West Darfur 237,000). 1.1 million people were 
reached with in-kind food assistance and 180,000 
people were reached with CBT. 
• The conditions in the Darfurs reflect a collapse of 
livelihoods and limited market functionality in 
many areas, meaning food is often physically 
unavailable. This has made WFP’s large-scale in-
kind assistance essential, particularly in IPC 4+ 
locations.  
• WFP reached 457,000 IDPs in Tawila as well as 
6,000 people in El Fasher, a famine-affected area.

• At the same time, where markets remain partially 
functional, CBT is supporting access by restoring 
purchasing power. WFP is beginning preparations 
to transition from in-kind assistance to CBT in 
areas of North Darfur. Large-scale registration 
and digitization commenced in April. This is being 
followed by a pilot in Tawila which is targeting 
100,000 beneficiaries in May. 
• Additionally, 177,000 people in West Darfur were 
supported through WFP’s Shock-Responsive 
Emergency Safety Net programme, helping 
mitigate the impacts of conflict, displacement, 
and economic shocks while strengthening 
resilience and access to basic services. 
• Given high malnutrition rates in the Darfurs and 
large inflows of displaced populations arriving in 
poor nutritional condition, nutrition support 
remains a critical life-saving intervention.  WFP 
reached 37,000 children and PBWG, supporting 
the treatment of MAM across all Darfur states.   
Khartoum 
• In April, WFP reached 160,000 people in 
Khartoum. 7,000 people were reached with in-
kind food assistance and 65,000 people were 
reached with CBT through WFP’s Shock-
Responsive Emergency Safety Net programme. 
• The operating environment remains fragile, with 
delays in food deliveries from Dongola and Al 
Dabbah and fuel shortages affecting operations 
in the state. 
• Food security has improved from peak conflict 
levels in Khartoum, but remains constrained by 
large-scale returns, limited infrastructure and 
slow economic recovery. Food is available in 
markets, but many households cannot afford it. 
WFP is responding to the situation by providing 
critical in-kind assistance to 59,000 people.  
• In parallel, 35,000 children and PBWG received 
support for the treatment for MAM, ensuring 
continued access to essential nutrition services. 
Northeast Sudan 
• Northeast Sudan hosts large, displaced 
populations and faces growing economic 
pressure.  
• In April, WFP reached 945,000 people in 
Northeast Sudan (Gedaref 85,000; Kassala 
215,000; Northern 116,000; Red Sea 24,000; River 
Nile 132,000 and White Nile 373,000). 59,000 
people were reached with in-kind food assistance 
and 754,000 people were reached with CBT. 
• April was marked by increasing fuel and 
commodity prices, as well as delays in truck 
clearances in Northern State. Despite this, WFP 
reached 114,000 people with in-kind food 
assistance.  
• Markets remained generally functioning in the 
region in April, allowing for WFP’s Shock-
Responsive Emergency Safety Net programme to 
reach 272,000 people through CBT, to be 
effective in restoring access to food and 
supporting local economies.  
• Emergency cash assistance also scaled up in 
White Nile, where CBPF funding enabled the 
rollout of cash-based assistance, reaching over 
320,000 people. 
• WFP’s nutrition response reached 67,000 children 
and PBWG to support the treatment of MAM, 
alongside 23,000 beneficiaries through Food-
Based Prevention of Malnutrition (FBPM). 
Nutrition assistance included 47,000 people 
reached in Northern State, 35,000 in River Nile, 
14,000 in Gedaref, 11,000 in Kassala, and 6,000 in 
White Nile. 
• A key feature of the Northeast region is the role 
of resilience programming, particularly in 
agriculture, which supports local food production 
and reduces long-term dependence on 
humanitarian assistance.

support to food systems transformations. Based

Wheat Production Project, WFP is supporting 
smallholder farmers with their harvests by

of April, 12 tractors were delivered in Northern 
State, with the remaining tractors to be

Southern Sudan 
• WFP sustained both lifesaving and resilience 
programming in April, reaching 237,000 people 
(Gezira 62,000 and Blue Nile 176,000) with 
assistance. 152,000 people were reached with in-
kind food assistance and 32,000 people were 
reached with CBT. 
• In the Southern States, WFP is both addressing 
immediate food needs while reducing future 
vulnerability. 
• In conflict-affected areas such as Blue Nile, access 
constraints and disrupted markets require 
continued in-kind food assistance. In April, WFP 
reached 152,000 people with in-kind food 
assistance, primarily in Blue Nile.  
• Additionally, a total of 53,000 children and PBWG 
received support for the treatment of MAM in 
Blue Nile and Gezira states.   
• In more stable and agriculturally viable areas 
such as Gezira, WFP implements resilience-
focused programming. In April, WFP reached 
13,000 people in Gezira through Food for Assets 
(FFA), contributing to livelihood recovery and 
community asset creation.

CLUSTER AND COMMON SERVICES 
Logistics Cluster (LC)  
• During the month, the Logistics Cluster 
received 1,181 m3 of humanitarian relief 
supplies at key common storage hubs in 
Tawila, Ad Dabbah, Kosti, Gedaref, and Port 
Sudan, representing a 142 percent increase 
compared to the previous month. Items 
included shelter, nutrition, education, 
protection, WASH, and health supplies, 
supporting 12 partners. 
• In April, common storage capacity in Tawila, 
North Darfur was nearly doubled – from 880m² 
to 1,600m² – enabling partners to pre-position 
multi-sectoral relief supplies ahead of the rainy 
season. 
• Through common transport services, the 
Logistics Cluster supported the movement of 
five trucks carrying 30 mt (101 m3) of relief 
supplies, including protection and health items, 
on behalf of two partners. 
UN Humanitarian Air Services (UNHAS) 
• In April 2026, UNHAS Sudan transported a total 
of 1,315 passengers and 2.38 metric tons of 
cargo across six regular destinations.  
• A total of 100 flights were operated in support 
of 79 humanitarian agencies.  
Emergency Telecommunications Cluster 
(ETC) 
• In April, a total of 67 sites were connected, with 
the highest concentrations in Port Sudan (39) and 
Kassala (21), alongside continued services in 
Kosti, Damazine, Gedaref, Dongola, and Atbara. 
Approximately 1,700 users relied on the network 
for essential operational communications. 
• ETC and UNDSS carried out a security 
communications assessment in Khartoum to 
guide a restoration plan in line with the UN’s 
phased return to Khartoum. 
Food Security Cluster (FSC) 
• In April, the FSL Cluster members have reached 
3.4 million individuals across all 18 states of 
Sudan, including 2.97 million with food assistance 
and 461,500 with livelihoods assistance.

FUNDING OVERVIEW 
• Severe resource constraints have forced WFP to 
undertake hyper-prioritization of beneficiaries 
across its operations. WFP reduced its annual 
target from 11.5 million people in 2025 to 8.5 
million in 2026.   
• Without urgent new funding, WFP will be forced 
to further scale back assistance - potentially 
reducing support by one million people per 
month by late 2026, primarily affecting those in 
the most severe levels of food insecurity (IPC 4+). 
Immediate funding is required to sustain 
life-saving assistance and protect critical 
humanitarian gains.
