Shashank Singh (56, 27b, 4x4, 4x6) attempted a late recovery, but Bhuvneshwar Kumar’s early strikes had rendered enough damage on Punjab Kings to seal Royal Challengers Bengaluru’s 23-run win in IPL 2026 at the HPCA Stadium on Sunday (May 17, 2026).
Shashank and Marcus Stoinis’ 67-run partnership for the sixth wicket came as a late tease but could not avert the inevitable. It started with a 20-run over from Suyash Sharma in which Shashank deposited three hits into the sight screen.
Stoinis, put down on 22 by Jacob Bethell off Krunal Pandya, peppered Bhuvneshwar and Rasikh Dar for three boundaries in the V, leaving PBKS 63 short with 24 balls in hand.
But Stoinis (37, 25b, 5x4) was pinned by a full toss from Josh Hazlewood and Punjab’s late charge fell to an anti-climactic end. The Aussie quick then gave away just seven off the penultimate over to seal RCB’s spot in the playoffs and leave PBKS on the brink of elimination.
Kings ultimately felt the pinch of their vaunted top-order collapsing. Chasing a daunting 223, Bhuvneshwar’s early swing accounted for Priyansh Arya and Prabhsimran Singh in successive overs. Shreyas Iyer was once again probed outside off and erred, falling caught behind off Rasikh.
In the first innings, Venkatesh Iyer lapped up a rare opportunity to notch up an IPL fifty after a year, thus getting RCB to 222 for four.
After going at less than run-a-ball through his first 10 deliveries, Venkatesh targeted the on-side to finish unbeaten on 73 (40b, 8x4, 4x6).
The ensuing lofty hits from Tim David (28 n.o., 12b, 2x4, 2x6) got RCB to an above-par score. But the groundwork was again laid by its ever-dependable talisman Virat Kohli (58, 37b, 4x4, 3x6).
Kohli spelled out his intent with a loft over covers off Ozmatullah Omarzai. Lockie Ferguson sprayed a length ball on his pads and saw it picked beyond the mid-wicket fence.
En route a 31-ball half-century, Kohli struck a crucial partnership with Devdutt Padikkal (45, 25b, 4x4, 3x6) worth 76 runs from just 41 balls. Kohli holed out to deep mid-wicket off Yuzvendra Chahal, but he had done enough to give Venkatesh a platform to launch.
